About Uberwrt

Uberwrt is a family of projects intended to resolve some endemic problems in networking today, and push the state of the art of edge networks and routers forward in various directions. Projects include tighter integration with DNSSEC (ISCwrt), wireless mesh networking (Wisp6), measurements of networking and censorship issues (Bismark), and others.

It is closely related to the ongoing debloat-testing efforts of Bufferbloat.net except that debloat-testing targets servers and laptops, not routers, directly.

About Cerowrt

Cerowrt is an Openwrt based platform intended for use by researchers, students and individuals interested in advancing the state of the art on the Internet, and in particular, those investigating the problems of latency under load, bufferbloat, wireless-n, QoS and the inter-relationships between various TCP algorithms.

A core goal for cerowrt is to provide a well understood platform, where researchers can perform tests with confidence that their results can be duplicated by others. (It has NOT achieved this goal yet!)

Cerowrt is a base for most of the other Uberwrt projects. It is kept small in size (under 6MB) so as to be compatible with more commonly available routers.

Cerowrt breaks with home router conventions in several ways. The first you are already seeing - it comes with an integral web server with which you can establish local web services. This is useful, for example, in providing a place where you can share data such as packet captures with others in your team, and a place to keep useful information when the Internet is down.

There are numerous other features all intended to help make insight into networking problems easier.

While we have tried very hard to produce a usable web interface for the normal use of Cerowrt as your primary home router (and do desire you use it as such and give us feedback!), some things, such as configuration of the web proxy, or alternate tcp algorithms require non-gui editing via ssh.

As this is a research platform primarily, there will be no long term support for this release. Subsequent releases will most likely require a complete reflashing of your router. We apologize for the inconvienence but lack funding to do more, and the state of the art and the problems we are trying to solve are rapidly moving targets that we would prefer to track closely.

Thanks for giving Cerowrt a try!
